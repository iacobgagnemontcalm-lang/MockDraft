How it works
VOR · ADP · Pick probability · DEF/K rule
The core idea
Raw rankings tell you a player is #25 overall — but that ignores two things: how many similar players are still available, and how badly you need that position. VOR accounts for both, giving every player a score that reflects their actual value to your specific roster right now. The score updates live as picks are made.
Step 1 — Dynamic replacement baseline
For each position the "replacement player" is the best freely available option after all 10 teams finish drafting. Unlike a fixed table, this baseline updates live as players are drafted — if 5 RBs are taken early, the replacement RB shifts down accordingly.
QB — 16th QB
RB — 41st RB
WR — 40th WR
TE — 17th TE
K — 11th K
DEF — 11th DEF
Step 2 — Roster slot weights
Not every roster spot matters equally. Your 1st RB starts every week — your 4th barely matters:
RB / WR
1st → 100% (starter)
2nd → 90% (starter)
3rd → 55% (FLEX)
4th → 25% (bench)
5th+ → 10% (handcuff)
QB / TE
1st → 100% (starter)
2nd → 50% (backup)
3rd+ → 0% (no value)
Single starter — depth drops off steeply
Step 3 — VOR formula
VOR = slot_weight × log(replECR / playerECR) × 100 × (1 + (1 − pickProb) × 0.9)
log(replECR / playerECR) × 100 — log-curve gap above the live replacement baseline. Compresses the scale so the difference between picks #1 and #10 isn't treated the same as #100 and #110.

slot_weight — how important is the Nth slot at this position on your roster right now.

(1 + (1 − pickProb) × 0.9) — scarcity multiplier. A player who is 99% likely to be gone by your next pick gets boosted up to ×1.9. A player who will definitely still be there gets ×1.0.
Pick probability %
The % bar on each player row answers: "what are the odds this player is still available when my next pick comes?" It uses a sigmoid curve centred on the player's weighted ADP, adjusted for picks already made. It updates when you switch teams in the dropdown — each team sees probability relative to their own next pick.
90–99%
Safe to wait a round
40–70%
Coin flip — take now or risk it
<20%
Almost certainly gone
Multi-source ADP & ± risk badge
The displayed ADP is a weighted average across up to 4 sources: FantasyPros ADP (w=1.0) · Fantasy Football Calculator (w=0.9) · MyFantasyLeague (w=0.8) · Sleeper (w=0.6, only when real draft ADP is available). The ± badge shows disagreement between sources — higher means more uncertainty:
±1–3 — Sources agree, low uncertainty
±4–8 — Moderate disagreement
±8+ — High disagreement, boom-or-bust
DEF / K drafting rule
Autopilot will never draft a DEF or K until a team has 13 total players rostered (keepers + draft picks combined). Human players can draft DEF/K at any time with no warning.

Risk quintiles — STD DEV badge
The coloured badge (✦◆●▲⬥) on each player shows their standard deviation rank within their position group. A WR ranked ⬥5 is risky compared to other WRs — not to all players.
✦1 — Experts agree
◆2 — Low variance
●3 — Moderate risk
▲4 — High variance
⬥5 — Boom or bust
💻 MoneyBall ↔ 🤠 Gut Feeling (autopilot)
Each team's autopilot has its own dial controlling how it picks:
💻 MoneyBall
Ranks by ECR. VOR in a tight window. Small STD DEV penalty. Pure data.
🤠 Gut Feeling
Ranks by ADP. Wider VOR window. Heavy STD DEV penalty. Boosts last year's roster and rookies.
Tip: Switch ECR ↔ ADP ↔ VOR in the header to change board sort order. A green ▲ means ECR ranks a player much higher than ADP — a potential value pick the market is sleeping on.
