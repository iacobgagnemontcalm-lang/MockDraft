How it works
iackScore · VOR · ECR steal · Tier cliff · Depth scarcity · 3-pick look-ahead
⚡ Top picks — the iackScore
The three cards rank available players using a composite score built from backtested signals. The VOR number (e.g. +8.3) on each card and board row is the raw marginal value — positive = above replacement. The final card ranking is driven by a 3-pick look-ahead that simulates your next three turns.
iackScore = max(VOR,0)×needMult + min(VOR,0)
           + (cliff×0.30 + depth×0.22) × |VOR| × 0.3
           + ECR steal bonus
3-pick value = iack(pick1) + 0.65×iack(pick2) + 0.42×iack(pick3)
Every signal was validated against 500+ simulated drafts using real 2026 ADP data. Run detection and demand signals were removed — they hurt more than they helped.
Signal 1 — VOR (value over replacement)
VOR answers: how much better is this player than the best freely available alternative at the same position? The replacement baseline updates live as players are drafted. Roster slot weights mean your 1st RB is worth more than your 4th:
RB / WR
1st → 100% · 2nd → 90% · 3rd → 55% · 4th → 25% · 5th+ → 10%
QB / TE
1st → 100% · 2nd → 50% · 3rd+ → 0%
Single starter — depth drops off steeply
Positional need (needMult) boosts RB/WR scores when you're behind expected count — but only scales positive VOR so it never inflates a bad pick.
Signal 2 — Scarcity urgency (% gone)
Each player has a % gone probability: the chance they're drafted before your next pick, based on a sigmoid curve around their ADP. High gone % is shown on the card tag:

<30% gone
Signal 3 — ECR steal ⭐ ▲
When a player's ECR (expert rank) is significantly better than their ADP (market rank), experts think the market is sleeping on them. The gap boosts their score — the bigger the gap, the more the app nudges you to take them before others wake up. A green ▲+N on the board row and ⭐ ECR steal tag on the card flag this. QB/TE steal bonuses are down-weighted since their ADP is more volatile.
Signal 4 — Tier cliff ▼ last T#
Players are grouped into tiers (T1, T2…). When a player is the last of his tier at his position — meaning the next available player there is in a worse tier — his score gets a cliff bonus. The board shows a dashed gold line between tiers. A ▼ last T4 tag means: take him now or drop to a materially worse tier.
Signal 5 — Depth scarcity
Counts how many elite players (tier 1–3) remain at each position. As that pool drains, the score for remaining elite players gets a step-up boost — the fewer left, the bigger the push to act. This captures the "musical chairs" dynamic at RB and WR in the middle rounds.
Signal 6 — 3-pick look-ahead
Instead of scoring each pick in isolation, the app simulates your next three turns:
For each candidate A:
 1. Score A now with your current roster state
 2. Find the best pick B likely available next turn — scored with updated needs (if you took an RB, your WR need is now higher)
 3. Find the best pick C likely available the turn after — scored with needs after A+B
 Total = iack(A) + 0.65×iack(B) + 0.42×iack(C)
The discount (0.65 per turn) reflects that future picks are less certain. The then → [player] +X.X line on each card shows the projected best pick for your next turn if you take that card now.
Roster nudge bar
The line below the panel header (e.g. 2RB · 1WR · 0QB · 0TE) tracks your current positional balance. If it turns into a warning like ⚠️ 0 RB through R5, the pool at that position is thinning fast — treat that as a hard signal to override the cards.

Multi-source ADP & ± risk badge
ADP is a weighted average across up to 4 sources: FantasyPros (w=1.0) · Fantasy Football Calculator (w=0.9) · MyFantasyLeague (w=0.8) · Sleeper (w=0.6). The ± badge shows disagreement between sources — higher = more uncertainty about where this player actually goes.
±1–3 — Sources agree
±4–8 — Moderate disagreement
±8+ — High disagreement / boom-or-bust
Risk badge ✦◆●▲⬥
Shows standard deviation rank within the position group. A WR ⬥5 is high-variance vs. other WRs, not all players.
✦1 — Experts agree
◆2 — Low variance
●3 — Moderate risk
▲4 — High variance
⬥5 — Boom or bust
💻 MoneyBall ↔ 🤠 Gut Feeling (autopilot)
Each bot team has its own slider controlling how it drafts:
💻 MoneyBall
Drafts by ECR. Tight VOR window. Small risk penalty. Pure data — snaps up ECR-undervalued players early.
🤠 Gut Feeling
Drafts by ADP. Wider VOR window. Heavy risk penalty. Boosts rookies & last year's holdovers — leaves ECR steals on the board longer.
DEF / K rule
Autopilot never drafts DEF or K until Round 14+ in redraft (or 10+ picks made in Dynasty). Human players can draft them any time.
Tip: The top picks panel is a shortlist — not a strict instruction. If the roster nudge bar is flashing a warning, trust positional balance over the card score. Switch ECR ↔ ADP ↔ VOR in the header to change board sort order.
